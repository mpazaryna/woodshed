# What is podiatry

Podiatry is a specialized field of medicine dedicated to the diagnoses and treatment of diseases, disorder, and injuries of the human foot and ankle.

Podiatric medicine is a branch of medicine that deals with the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of injuries, diseases and other conditions of the lower leg, ankle and foot. Doctors who practice podiatric medicine aim to help people regain mobility and movement so they can walk and run normally. They also contribute to the body's overall health. This is because the lower extremities have an intricate relationship with the rest of the human body and can play a significant role in identifying conditions such as cardiovascular disease and diabetes before signs appear in other areas of the body.
